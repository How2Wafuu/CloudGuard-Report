# Problem

As cyber threats grow more sophisticated, the demand for comprehensive Security Information and Event Management (SIEM) and Extended Detection and Response (XDR) systems has never been greater. However, the current cybersecurity market creates a significant barrier to entry for many organizations.

### 1. Cost-Prohibitive Infrastructure

Traditional enterprise-grade SIEM solutions require massive on-premise infrastructure or expensive cloud computing resources. Continuously streaming, storing, and analyzing raw network logs in real-time demands high bandwidth and petabytes of storage, pricing out organizations with limited IT budgets.

### 2. Operational Complexity

Advanced threat detection systems are notoriously complex to deploy and maintain. They typically require a dedicated 24/7 Security Operations Center (SOC) staffed by highly specialized security engineers to write detection rules, manage the infrastructure, and tune out false positives.

### 3. The SMB Vulnerability Gap

Because of high price tags and operational overhead, Small to Medium-sized Businesses (SMBs) are often forced to rely on fragmented, basic security tools (such as standard firewalls or standalone antivirus software). This leaves them highly vulnerable to complex attacks such as:

* **"Low and Slow" Attacks:** Intrusions that happen over long periods to evade basic detection.
* **Advanced Persistent Threats (APTs):** Sophisticated, sustained cyberattacks.
* **Insider Threats:** Malicious or negligent actions taken by authorized users within the network.

**The Conclusion:** High-end cybersecurity is currently treated as a luxury for massive enterprises. There is a critical need for a solution that delivers enterprise-standard automated threat detection without the crushing infrastructure and operational costs.
