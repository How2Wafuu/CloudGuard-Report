# Problem

As cyber threats become increasingly sophisticated, the need for comprehensive Security Information and Event Management (SIEM) and Extended Detection and Response (XDR) systems has never been higher. However, the current cybersecurity market presents a significant barrier to entry.

### 1. Cost-Prohibitive Infrastructure

Traditional enterprise-grade SIEM solutions require massive on-premise infrastructure or expensive cloud computing resources. Continuously streaming, storing, and analyzing raw network logs in real-time demands high bandwidth and petabytes of storage, pricing out organizations with limited IT budgets \[1].

### 2. Operational Complexity

Advanced threat detection systems are notoriously complex to deploy and maintain. They typically require a dedicated 24/7 Security Operations Center (SOC) staffed by highly specialized security engineers to write detection rules, manage the infrastructure, and tune out false positives.

### 3. The SMB Vulnerability Gap

Because of the high price tags and operational overhead, **Small to Medium-sized Businesses (SMBs)** are often forced to rely on fragmented, basic security tools. This leaves them highly vulnerable to complex attacks. In 2024 alone, the NCSA reported over 22,000 cyber incidents in Thailand, representing a 35% year-over-year increase primarily targeting under-secured organizations \[2].

* **"Low and Slow" Attacks:** Intrusions that happen over long periods to evade basic detection.
* **Advanced Persistent Threats (APTs):** Sophisticated, sustained cyberattacks.

**The Conclusion:** High-end cybersecurity is currently treated as a luxury for massive enterprises. There is a critical need for a solution that delivers automated threat detection without the crushing infrastructure and operational costs.
